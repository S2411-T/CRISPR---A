Mechanistic Optimization Logic Summary

The optimization suite analyzes single-nucleotide base properties across the selected spacer coordinate grid. When risk factors (such as extreme high-accessibility chromatin states, structural sequence homologies, or dangerous out-of-frame 5'-NGG distribution patterns) are identified, the engine computes alternative nearby coordinate offsets. It weights single base substitutions across the non-seed sequence region, optimizing the GC ratio bounds to secure lower absolute binding free energies ($\Delta G$). This stabilizes the Cas9/sgRNA complex structure, suppressing non-specific off-target interactions at remote human genomic loci.

Overview

SafeCRISPR AI is a machine learning platform for predicting CRISPR off-target effects. It integrates curated genomic reference sources and ensemble model outputs to prioritize guide designs with minimal off-target risk and maximal sequence fidelity.

The dashboard is organized into clinical workflow stages: input parameters, live predictive metrics, mismatch fidelity visualization, off-target risk heatmaps, and per-model ensemble channels.

SafeCRISPR Score™

The SafeCRISPR Score™ is a composite index derived from off-target probability, seed region integrity, PAM quality, prediction confidence, mismatch profile, chromatin protection, and genomic context.

A score above 75 represents a Tier I clinical-grade candidate. Scores between 45 and 75 are Tier II with moderate risk. Scores below 45 are Tier III and require immediate reassessment.

Model Architecture

This system ensembles transformer-based sequence encoding, convolutional feature extraction, gradient-boosted tree classifiers, and randomized decision forests to produce a consensus prediction.

Each channel contributes independent evidence: DNABERT extracts sequence context, DeepCNN captures local signal patterns, XGBoost models structured features, and Random Forest provides robustness against noisy guide variants.

Training Data

Model training is based on contemporary genomic risk datasets such as GUIDE-Seq, CRISPResso benchmarks, dbSNP variants, ClinVar pathogenicity annotations, and GRCh38 reference sequences.

The architecture also integrates experimentally validated on-target/off-target outcomes to improve model calibration and reduce false positives in therapeutic design.

Running an Analysis

Populate the target DNA sequence, species, chromosome locus, and guide array. Then submit the pipeline to compute ensemble metrics, visualize alignment fidelity, and dynamically generate recommendations.

The system preserves results locally and enables clinical teams to review the highest-scoring guide while comparing alternate spacer candidates.

Limitations & Disclaimers

SafeCRISPR AI is a machine learning platform for predicting CRISPR off target effects. It uses a Transfromer-based architecture trained on experimentally validated datasets to predict the likelihood of unintended DNA modifications. It does not replace orthogonal experimental validation such as NGS, GUIDE-Seq, or clinical-grade genomic sequencing.

Use these outputs to prioritize candidate spacers, not as the final therapeutic decision. Always confirm any design in a wet-lab validation workflow and regulatory review.
